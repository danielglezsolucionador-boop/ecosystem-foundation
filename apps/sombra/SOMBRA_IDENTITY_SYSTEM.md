## Identity Doctrine

This file documents governance for SOMBRA identity-related architecture.

It does not define operational impersonation tactics, infiltration steps, platform evasion, language simulation, account creation procedures, infrastructure separation tactics, forum behavior, or any executable identity workflow.

Any future work related to operational identities is restricted and requires:

- CEO authorization.
- CEREBRO routing.
- Legal review.
- Black box audit.
- Explicit mission scope.
- Jurisdiction assessment.
- No customer-facing exposure.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## Section T5.1 — Identity Governance Architecture

An identity record, if ever authorized and implemented, must be governed as a restricted asset.

Required non-actionable governance fields:

- Internal identity ID.
- Internal codename.
- Status.
- Authorization record.
- Legal review status.
- Mission scope.
- Risk score.
- Last review timestamp.
- Black box audit reference.
- Retirement status.

Forbidden in public or customer-facing documentation:

- Usernames.
- Operational communities.
- Infrastructure details.
- Connection methods.
- Payment artifacts.
- Private communications.
- Any information that reveals source methods.

Implementation status: not implemented.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## Section T5.2 — Authorization Protocol

No identity-related operation may begin without explicit authorization.

Required approval chain:

1. CEREBRO prepares justification.
2. Legal and jurisdictional risk is assessed.
3. CEO approves or rejects.
4. Approved scope is written to black box audit.
5. SOMBRA remains within scope.

Hard blockers:

- No unauthorized access.
- No credential misuse.
- No extortion.
- No commerce with malicious actors.
- No operational activity outside approved scope.
- No public attribution to SOMBRA.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## Section T5.3 — Language And Output Safety

SOMBRA is not a chatbot and does not communicate with users.

Any future language generation related to restricted operations must be:

- Approved by CEO.
- Routed through CEREBRO.
- Logged in black box audit.
- Limited to authorized scope.
- Reviewed for legal and operational risk.

This repository does not document how to imitate community language, evade detection, or generate posts for restricted environments.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## Section T5.4 — Compartmentalization Governance

Compartmentalization is documented here as a defensive governance requirement only.

Governance requirements:

- No cross-client data contamination.
- No cross-mission data leakage.
- No customer-facing operational metadata.
- No source-method exposure.
- Strict access control.
- Audit trail for all restricted records.
- Immediate CEREBRO notification on suspected compromise.

Implementation status: not implemented.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## Section T5.5 — Lifecycle Governance

Any future restricted identity asset must have a lifecycle.

Lifecycle states:

- Proposed.
- Awaiting CEO authorization.
- Approved.
- Active within approved scope.
- Paused.
- Cooling.
- Retired.
- Compromised.
- Archived CEO-only.

Lifecycle rules:

- No indefinite operation without review.
- No scope expansion without authorization.
- No deletion of audit history.
- Compromise suspicion triggers freeze and CEREBRO notification.
- CEO-only archive preserves records without public exposure.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## Section T5.6 — Risk Register

Identity-related operations carry elevated risk.

Risk classes:

- Legal risk.
- Source exposure risk.
- Client confidentiality risk.
- Ecosystem reputation risk.
- Counterintelligence risk.
- False intelligence risk.
- Overreach risk.

Each risk must have:

- Severity.
- Likelihood.
- Mitigation.
- Approval status.
- Audit reference.
- Owner.
